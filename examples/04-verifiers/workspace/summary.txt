Supervised machine learning is a type of artificial intelligence where a computer learns to make predictions by studying examples that have already been labeled with the correct answers. For instance, you might show it thousands of photos labeled "cat" or "dog" so it learns to tell them apart. Once trained, the model can look at new, unseen examples and make accurate predictions on its own. It is called "supervised" because a human provides the correct answers during training, guiding the learning process.
